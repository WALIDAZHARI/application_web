Why Casablanca ?

5 reasons to visit Casablanca.

A city facing the ocean

Enjoy the mildness of its warm and sunny climate all year round and take a walk along the corniche. On the large beach you can practice a large number of water activities such as surfing.

In the footsteps of the past

With its rich historical past, Casablanca includes many historical monuments such as the Mahkama du Pacha highlighted by its Hispano-Moorish architecture. Also take a walk in the old medina or in the direction of the rocky islet of Sidi Abderrahman to discover chapters of the history of the city.

The happiness of being greedy

As in all the cities of the kingdom, here the food is simply delicious. We enjoy a simple fried fish or eggplant sandwiches sold in the medina, or even the essential almond pastries.

To the rhythm of the festivals

In April, the Jazzablanca festival attracts musicians from all over the world. The Le Boulevard festival for young musicians, nicknamed L'Boulevard, transforms the city center into a gigantic performance hall, while the Anfa festival transforms the main beach into an open-air concert hall at the end of July.

An ideal location for visiting other places

Take advantage of the geographical location of Casablanca to discover other wonders of the country. You can easily go to Rabat as well as to El Jadida, or even go on camel treks in the Bedouin country.

Morocco Mall

Discover the first "Retailtainment" Mall in Africa and the Region. Enjoy the best of shopping and entertainment in an incomparable place that represents one of Morocco's modern showcases, symbolizing its diversity, openness and generosity. A real destination for nationals as well as for tourists or passing visitors, the Morocco Mall houses a full range of services: fashion, beauty, luxury, culture, technology, health, decoration, food, equipment, crafts, services, leisure, relaxation , catering... Extending over 10 hectares with 70,000 m2 of commercial space on 3 levels and 350 sales areas, the Morocco Mall brings together more than 600 brands from various universes.

Sindibad Park

SINDIBAD PARK is a subsidiary of SINDIBAD BEACH RESORT and is located in the heart of Ain Diab, in Casablanca, the park covers 32 ha and includes 3 different universes: An amusement park, An animal park, and a forest recreational. The park has more than 20 surprising attractions, more than thirty animal species, souvenir shops, themed restaurants, as well as sandwich shops. Diversified entertainment is also offered throughout the day for even more of a change of scenery. Entrance tickets to the par vary according to the size of the visitors and are available in weekly rates and Weekend/holiday/summer vacation rates. Attractions are payable either individually (in diamonds) or as a package (entry + attractions).

The AnfaPlace Mall is a shopping center located at " boulevard de la corniche de Aïn Diab in Casablanca ", the economic capital of Morocco and was inaugurated on February 8, 2013. Composed of more than 30,000 square meters of commercial area, the shopping center has a ground floor and two floors of commercial spaces, the first being mainly dedicated to shops and various stores and the second to fast food. , and has two levels of underground parking, offering over a thousand parking spaces spread over two dedicated underground levels.

Hassan 2 Mosque

After the mosque in Mecca, the Hassan II mosque in Casablanca is the second highest religious monument on the planet. With a minaret culminating at 210 meters above sea level, the mosque is indeed gigantic in size. Erected between 1986 and 1993, the Hassan II mosque was built two-thirds over the sea. It can accommodate more than 100,000 worshippers, it covers a total area of ​​twenty thousand square meters and it has an impressive sunroof. Designed by Michel Pinseau, the Hassan II mosque is also an architectural gem. The monument is finely decorated with marble, zelliges, carved wooden panels and it has a laser (with a range of 30 km) constantly pointed towards Mecca.

Marche Central

The Central Market was designed by Pierre Bousquet, and construction was completed in 1917, on the site of the Casablanca Fair of 1915. The Central Market was the most important marketplace in Casablanca's European ville nouvelle. The Moroccan nationalist resistance fighter Muhammad Zarqtuni bombed the Central Market on December 24, 1953, after French forces forced Sultan Muhammad VI into exile on August 20, 1953 — which was Eid al-Adha.

Place des Nations-Unies

Place des Nations-Unies, formerly called Place de France, is a square in the center of Casablanca. It was laid out by the French architect and urban planner Henri Prost in 1915 and served as a link between the old medina and the new city center.

Place Mohamed V

Mohammed V Square (Arabic: ساحة محمد الخامس) is a public square of historical and symbolic significance located in central Casablanca, Morocco. It was established in 1916 at the beginning of the French protectorate in Morocco under Resident-general Hubert Lyautey, on a design by architects Henri Prost and Joseph Marrast.

Église du Sacré-Cœur

The Church of the Sacred Heart ( Arabic : كاتدرائية القلب المقدس) is a former Catholic shrine in the city of Casablanca, Morocco . It was built from 1930 by the architect Paul Tournon 1, winner of the Prix de Rome, in a style combining Gothic aesthetics and Art Deco. It is surrounded by a vast public garden, the Parc de la Ligue Arabe .

Villa des Arts

Located at 30 Boulevard Brahim Roudani in Casablanca, the Villa des Arts Museum is a place that is retro, nostalgic and profoundly modern at the same time. And for good reason, this museum exhibits contemporary Moroccan art in a superb renovated 1930s villa. Belonging to the ONA Foundation, the Museum of the Villa des Arts is a major cultural place in the city of Casablanca.

Palais Royale

In Casablanca, in the Habous district, is one of the royal palaces of Morocco. Discover this extraordinary place, built at the beginning of the 20th century, and consider renting a car in Casablanca to go and admire it up close.
The Royal Palace of Casablanca is certainly the most beautiful monument in the city. Built in the 1920s by the Pertuzio brothers, this building has an Arab-Muslim architectural style while being very modern. At the heart of the palace, Mediterranean-style gardens designed by the architect Forestier make the place even more sumptuous.

Mehkama du Pacha

Mahkamat al-Pasha (Arabic: مَحْكَمَة الباشا "the pasha's courthouse," French: Mahkama du Pacha) is an administrative building constructed 1941-1942 in the Hubous neighborhood of Casablanca, Morocco. The complex serves or has served as a courthouse, residence of the pasha (governor), parliamentary reception hall, and jail.
